Market Research 2025

Global AI Job Market & Salary Trends

Analyzing ~15,000 AI job records to uncover salary trends, skill demand patterns, and cross-industry adoption — revealing where AI hiring is actually growing beyond tech.

The Challenge

The AI job market is one of the fastest-evolving labor markets globally, yet data is fragmented across platforms and geographies. Job seekers lack clarity on which skills command premium salaries, and companies struggle to benchmark compensation. Beyond tech giants, which industries are genuinely adopting AI talent?

~15K Job Records
Multi Industries
Global Geography
5+ Dimensions

Approach & Methodology

  • Data Collection & Cleaning: Worked with a real-world dataset of ~15,000 AI job postings. Standardized job titles, skill taxonomies, and salary formats across different markets.
  • Multi-Dimensional Modeling: Built analysis across 5 key dimensions — skills required, geography, company size, job type (full-time/contract/remote), and industry sector.
  • Cross-Industry Analysis: Deliberately looked beyond traditional tech companies to identify AI adoption patterns in Retail, Media, Automotive, Healthcare, and Financial sectors.
  • Salary Benchmarking: Created normalized salary comparisons controlling for geography, experience level, and company size to identify truly high-value skills vs. location premiums.
  • Interactive Dashboard: Built a Power BI solution allowing users to slice data by any combination of dimensions for personalized insights.

Key Insights

The most surprising finding: AI hiring growth rate in Retail and Automotive sectors now outpaces traditional tech companies. These industries are in the early adoption phase, creating opportunities for data professionals to enter less competitive markets with strong growth trajectories.

Skill-wise, the combination of domain expertise + ML fundamentals commands higher salaries than pure ML engineering in many contexts. Companies are paying a premium for people who can bridge the gap between data science and business strategy.

Geographically, remote-first AI roles have compressed salary bands — creating arbitrage opportunities for talent in lower cost-of-living areas with strong skills.
